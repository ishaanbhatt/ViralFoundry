# Strategy

## Thesis

The path to thousands of dollars per month is not a single viral account. It is a portfolio of content experiments where each account has:

- A clear niche and audience.
- Original or licensed inputs.
- A monetization route that does not depend only on platform payout.
- Fast iteration on hooks, retention, posting windows, and formats.
- Analytics that feed future idea generation.

The system should behave like a small autonomous media lab. It should test aggressively, but it should not behave like spam automation or an account farm.

## Initial Portfolio

### 1. Setup Under Budget

Primary goal: fastest monetization.

Format:

- Product comparisons under a strict budget.
- "Buy this, skip that" clips.
- AI tool and creator gear explainers.
- Desk, streaming, study, and productivity setup tips.

Revenue:

- Affiliate links.
- Brand sponsorships.
- Digital guides.
- Newsletter or storefront.

Why it can win:

- Buyer intent is explicit.
- Saves and shares matter.
- The content can be made without rights-heavy source material.

### 2. The Archive at 3:17

Primary goal: owned IP.

Format:

- Original AI horror micro-series.
- Recurring town, archive, files, rules, and viewer choices.
- Voice, captions, atmospheric visuals, and comment-led sequels.

Revenue:

- YouTube compilations.
- Patreon or paid bonus archive.
- Story collections.
- Merch.
- Sponsorships in horror/gaming.

Why it can win:

- Original lore compounds.
- AI visuals fit the genre.
- The channel can become an IP asset instead of disposable traffic.

### 3. One Minute Internet Mysteries

Primary goal: defensible audience growth.

Format:

- Lost media.
- Weird internet history.
- Failed apps and games.
- Timeline-style explainers.

Revenue:

- YouTube long-form bridge.
- Sponsorships.
- Newsletter.
- Paid research packs.

Why it can win:

- Higher trust than generic facts.
- Better long-form bridge.
- Easier to cite and package as educational entertainment.

### 4. Clip Doctor

Primary goal: service and revenue-share business.

Format:

- Authorized streamer clips with commentary.
- Stakes-first setup before the clip.
- Short breakdown of why the moment worked.

Revenue:

- Editing retainers.
- Revenue share.
- Sponsorship packages.
- Agency offer.

Strict rule:

- No unauthorized streamer clipping. This lane only exists with explicit permission and stored terms.

## Operating Plan

First 30 days:

- Run 2 primary channels, not every idea at once.
- Produce 60 to 90 planned posts across TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts.
- Keep all public posting owner-reviewed until the system has logs, policy gates, and stable provider integrations.
- Test five hook styles per pillar.
- Track results by content lineage: idea, hook, script, voice, visual style, posting time, platform, and outcome.

First channels:

- `setup-under-budget` for monetization.
- `archive-317` for owned IP.

Second wave:

- `internet-mysteries` after the citation and research workflow exists.
- `clip-doctor` only after permission workflows and creator outreach templates exist.

## Success Metrics

Content metrics:

- Three-second hold proxy.
- Average view duration.
- Completion proxy.
- Saves and shares.
- Comments per view.
- Follower delta.
- Revenue per thousand views.

Business metrics:

- Affiliate clicks and conversion.
- Sponsor replies.
- Email signups.
- Paid memberships.
- Creator clipping contracts.

System metrics:

- Cost per generated post.
- Cost per published post.
- Policy block rate.
- Owner review time.
- Render failure rate.
- Metrics collection lag.

## Kill Rules

Suppress a format after 10 posts if:

- It repeatedly fails to earn distribution across platforms.
- It triggers policy or originality risk.
- It has no credible monetization route.
- It requires more editing effort than higher-value lanes.

Double down when:

- A hook family wins at least three times.
- Saves or shares beat the channel baseline.
- The format creates direct revenue intent.
- Viewers ask for sequels or product links.

## Revenue Reality

The system can plausibly reach thousands per month only if it layers revenue:

- Creator payouts for eligible original content.
- Affiliate revenue from product channels.
- Sponsorships from niche authority.
- Owned IP monetization from horror and documentary channels.
- Service revenue from authorized clipping.

Short-form ad or rewards payouts alone are too volatile to be the whole plan.
